In a few weeks, our family is boarding a Disney cruise ship in a concierge suite. I've been on Disney cruises before. I have never been in concierge before.

My mother-in-law Barbara — who is currently somewhere on the Pacific Crest Trail eating a meal she dehydrated herself — booked this one. She tends to do things like that.

Since we sail May 23rd and I am constitutionally incapable of showing up somewhere without knowing what's going to happen, I've spent an embarrassing number of hours researching Disney concierge. Here's what I've learned.

## What concierge actually is (and isn't)

Disney Concierge isn't just a bigger room. It's a separate level of service that includes:

- **A dedicated concierge lounge** — private space, snacks and drinks throughout the day, a bar in the evening, and a team of hosts who handle your requests
- **Priority boarding** — you board before general guests, which with young kids is genuinely significant
- **Priority dining reservations** — you get earlier access to book specialty restaurants, which matters a lot on Disney ships
- **Priority port excursion booking** — same idea
- **Concierge hosts** — actual humans whose entire job is to solve your problems. Not cast members with a lot of other responsibilities. People specifically there for you.
- **Access to concierge-only areas** — sun deck, lounge, etc. depending on the ship

What it's not: it's not a separate ship. You're still on the same vessel as everyone else. The main difference is a layer of support, priority access, and a home base that's a little quieter.

## The lounge

This is the thing I'm most curious about. From what I've read, the concierge lounge has:

- Complimentary alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages during evening hours (and non-alcoholic throughout the day)
- Snacks and small bites that cycle throughout the day
- A team of hosts who know your name by day two
- A comfortable space that's meaningfully less chaotic than the rest of the ship

With two kids aged 3 and 5, "meaningfully less chaotic" is basically all I want from a vacation.

## What I'm planning to do on embarkation day

Everything I've read says the same thing: **embarkation day is when concierge guests have the biggest advantage**. Here's my plan:

1. Board as early as possible with the concierge group
2. Head straight to the lounge to meet our hosts
3. Use that first hour — before general boarding is complete and the ship gets busy — to nail down any specialty restaurant reservations we didn't get in advance
4. Let the kids run while we do the boring logistics

## Questions I still have

I'm going to be honest: there are things I haven't nailed down yet that I'll report back on after we sail.

- **How does it work with a large group?** We're not traveling solo — we've got in-laws, a couple brothers-in-law, and a 1-year-old in the mix. Do the concierge benefits extend to adjacent staterooms? How does dining logistics work when you're coordinating 8+ people?
- **What do the hosts actually proactively do vs. what do I have to ask for?** I've seen conflicting reports on this.
- **Kids' clubs and concierge** — does concierge status change anything about the Oceaneer Club/Lab experience?

## What we're sailing

Two back-to-back Disney sailings. I'll be posting the full itinerary details closer to departure, but I can say: it's May, it's Disney, there's a concierge suite, and there will be at least four children aboard our family group who range from 1 to 5 years old.

It's going to be a lot. It's going to be great. And I'm going to document all of it.
